The Story
Why I Built This Tool
In the development of next-generation AR glasses, the conflict between high-performance hardware (chips, batteries, heat sinks) and long-term human comfort is the primary barrier to mass adoption. Traditional design processes often rely on “guess-and-check” physical prototyping — slow, costly, and unable to account for the complex biomechanical interaction between rigid hardware and soft human tissue.

I designed this Human-Centered Computational Tool to bridge the gap between industrial design and biomechanical engineering. By integrating Real-time Parametric Modeling with FEA (Finite Element Analysis), this tool allows designers to treat “Comfort” as a quantifiable data point rather than a subjective feeling — enabling Analysis-before-Design, so that every gram of electronic weight is strategically placed to minimise pressure on the user’s nose and ears.
The Solution
What It Solves
🧬
Static vs. Dynamic Fit
Traditional frames ignore the deformation of soft tissue. This tool simulates non-linear skin compression to predict pain points before a single prototype is printed.
⚖️
Weight Distribution
Solves the “Front-Heavy” problem of AR glasses by identifying the Pareto optimal position for batteries and sensors to balance the centre of gravity.
⚡
Rapid Iteration
Reduces R&D cycle by linking simulation results directly to design parameters, enabling thousands of virtual try-ons in seconds rather than weeks of prototyping.
Technical Pipeline
How It Works
1
3D / 4D Anatomical Data Acquisition
Capture high-fidelity facial scans. Use ML models to segment the mesh and predict soft tissue thickness based on a library of anatomical data.
2
Parametric Hardware Integration
Define the glasses frame and electronic modules (SoC, Battery) as a linked parametric system in Grasshopper / Python for full design control.
3
Biomechanical Simulation (FEA)
Link the model to Abaqus or FEBio. Perform soft-body contact simulation to calculate contact pressure (kPa) at the nose bridge and temple tips.
4
Algorithmic Optimisation (Genetic Algorithms)
Run GA to iterate through module placement scenarios. The solver minimises peak pressure while maintaining a balanced centre of gravity (CG).
5
Web-Based Design Dashboard
Visualise results in a real-time WebApp — providing the designer with a live Comfort Score and automated Design Insights to drive the next iteration.
